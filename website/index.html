The Hawksbill Turtle

The hawksbill sea turtle is a type of sea turtle that is considered critically endangered by the IUCN. As the turtles possess a distinctive pattern of overlapping scales on their colored and patterned shells, they have been hunted for thousands of years. According to the World Wildlife Federation, the population of hawksbill turtles in the world has declined by over 80% over the last century with around 8,000 left today.
